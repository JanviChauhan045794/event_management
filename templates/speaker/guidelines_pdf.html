Speaker Guidelines

Essential information to help you prepare and deliver successful presentations.

Before the Event

Preparation Timeline

Timeframe Action Items
4 weeks before
  • Confirm your participation and speaking slot
  • Book travel and accommodation if needed
  • Begin preparing your presentation content
2 weeks before
  • Finalize your presentation slides
  • Practice your presentation multiple times
  • Submit your presentation materials
1 week before
  • Review event schedule and logistics
  • Prepare answers for potential Q&A questions
  • Test any demos or technical components
1 day before
  • Pack all necessary items
  • Get a good night's sleep
  • Review your presentation one final time

Material Submission

All presentation materials must be submitted at least 48 hours before your scheduled speaking time:

  • Presentation slides (PowerPoint or PDF format)
  • Any handouts for attendees
  • Demo files or code samples
  • Speaker bio and headshot (if not already provided)

Technical Requirements

Presentation Format

  • Aspect Ratio: 16:9 (widescreen)
  • File Formats: PowerPoint (.pptx) or PDF (.pdf)
  • Video Formats: MP4 (H.264 codec)
  • Audio: Embedded in presentation or separate MP3 files
If your presentation includes videos, please also provide them as separate files in case of embedding issues.

Available Equipment

  • Projector and screen (HDMI connection)
  • Microphone (lapel or handheld, based on room size)
  • Presenter remote/clicker
  • Podium or lectern
  • Basic sound system for audio playback

Presentation Tips

Slide Design

  • Use large, readable fonts (minimum 24pt)
  • Keep text concise - use bullet points
  • Include relevant images and diagrams
  • Use high-contrast color schemes
  • Include slide numbers

Delivery Tips

  • Practice thoroughly - Rehearse your presentation multiple times
  • Speak clearly - Maintain a moderate pace
  • Make eye contact - Connect with different sections of the audience
  • Use natural gestures - Let your body language enhance your message
  • Manage your time - Stay within your allotted time
  • Engage the audience - Consider incorporating interactive elements

Q&A Sessions

Preparing for Q&A

  • Anticipate potential questions and prepare concise answers
  • If you don't know the answer, it's okay to say so - offer to follow up later
  • Keep answers brief to allow for multiple questions
  • Listen carefully to the entire question before responding
  • Repeat questions for the audience if needed

Material Submission

All presentation materials must be submitted at least 48 hours before your scheduled speaking time. This includes:

  • Presentation slides (PowerPoint or PDF format)
  • Any handouts for attendees
  • Demo files or code samples
  • Speaker bio and headshot (if not already provided)

Materials can be uploaded through the Presentation Materials section of your speaker dashboard.

Day of the Event

Arrival Time

Please arrive at least 60 minutes before your scheduled speaking time. This will allow you to:

  • Check in at the speaker registration desk
  • Test your presentation on the venue's equipment
  • Meet with the event coordinator or moderator
  • Familiarize yourself with the stage and equipment
What to Bring
Essential Items
  • Laptop with your presentation
  • Power adapter and any necessary dongles
  • Backup copy of your presentation (USB drive)
  • Business cards
  • Water bottle
Recommended Items
  • Presenter remote/clicker
  • Notes or speaking outline
  • Phone charger
  • Throat lozenges
  • Business cards

Presentation Tips

Slide Design
Do
  • Use large, readable fonts (minimum 24pt)
  • Keep text concise - use bullet points
  • Include relevant images and diagrams
  • Use high-contrast color schemes
  • Include slide numbers
Don't
  • Overcrowd slides with text
  • Use complex animations or transitions
  • Include low-resolution images
  • Use more than 2-3 fonts
  • Read directly from slides
Delivery Tips
  • Practice thoroughly - Rehearse your presentation multiple times to ensure smooth delivery and proper timing.
  • Speak clearly and at a moderate pace - Be mindful of your speaking speed, especially when explaining complex concepts.
  • Make eye contact - Connect with different sections of the audience throughout your presentation.
  • Use natural gestures - Let your body language enhance your message, but avoid excessive movements.
  • Manage your time - Practice with a timer to ensure you can complete your presentation within the allotted time.
  • Engage the audience - Consider incorporating polls, questions, or brief activities to maintain audience interest.

Technical Requirements

Presentation Format
  • Aspect Ratio: 16:9 (widescreen)
  • File Formats: PowerPoint (.pptx) or PDF (.pdf)
  • Video Formats: MP4 (H.264 codec)
  • Audio: Embedded in presentation or separate MP3 files
If your presentation includes videos, please also provide them as separate files in case of embedding issues.
Available Equipment

The following equipment will be provided at the venue:

  • Projector and screen (HDMI connection)
  • Microphone (lapel or handheld, based on room size)
  • Presenter remote/clicker
  • Podium or lectern
  • Basic sound system for audio playback

If you require any additional equipment or have special technical needs, please contact the event coordinator at least one week before the event.

Q&A Sessions

Preparing for Q&A

Most presentations will include a Q&A session. Here are some tips to prepare:

  • Anticipate potential questions and prepare concise answers
  • If you don't know the answer, it's okay to say so - offer to follow up later
  • Keep answers brief to allow for multiple questions
  • Listen carefully to the entire question before responding
  • Repeat questions for the audience if the questioner doesn't use a microphone
Managing Difficult Questions

Occasionally, you may receive challenging or confrontational questions. Here's how to handle them:

  • Remain calm and professional
  • Acknowledge the question's validity, even if you disagree with its premise
  • Provide a balanced response based on facts
  • If the question is off-topic, briefly address it and redirect to the presentation's focus
  • For very specific or technical questions, offer to continue the discussion after the session

After the Event

Follow-Up Actions
  • Complete any promised follow-ups from Q&A sessions
  • Share your presentation materials if appropriate
  • Connect with attendees who provided business cards or contact information
  • Review feedback from your session
  • Send a thank-you note to the event organizers
Feedback and Improvement

After your presentation, you'll receive feedback from attendees through the event platform. This feedback is valuable for improving future presentations.

You can access your feedback reports in the Feedback section of your speaker dashboard.
